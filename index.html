Project Overview

Good Luck Furniture, a handcrafted furniture business in Prayagraj, faced critical operational bottlenecks. This project utilized historical sales data (2023-2025) to identify patterns and propose actionable business solutions.

Diagnostic

Analyzed 80+ sales transactions and supplier logs to pinpoint the root causes of revenue loss during peak seasons.

Strategy

Developed a "Bundle & Clear" strategy and a seasonal inventory plan to optimize stock levels and cash flow.

Impact

Forecasted a ₹1.6 Lakh profit potential for the 2025 peak season and identified key supply chain bottlenecks.

Core Business Challenges

Seasonal Stock Shortages

The business suffers from "Boom & Bust" cycles. Profits spike 10x during Oct-Nov, but poor planning leads to stockouts and lost revenue.

Supply Chain Disruptions

Over-dependence on a single supplier, "Sheesham Crafts Inc.", causes average production delays of 5.5 days.

Underperforming Portfolio

Capital is tied up in dead stock (e.g., Rocking Chairs) while high-demand items (Beds, Custom sets) are often unavailable.

Data Analysis & Insights

Pareto Principle in Product Mix

Profitability is highly concentrated. Just two categories account for nearly 64% of total business profit.

Beds & Custom 63.8%
All Other Products 36.2%

Supplier Performance Gap

Data identified "Sheesham Crafts Inc." as the primary bottleneck compared to reliable suppliers like "Bharat Hardware".

  • Bharat Hardware 0.3 days delay
  • Prayag Timbers 1.2 days delay
  • Sheesham Crafts Inc. 5.5 days delay

Strategic Recommendations

1

Seasonal Inventory Plan

Proactively increase procurement of top 10 products by 30% before September. Target the forecasted ₹1.67L peak profit in 2025.

2

"Bundle & Clear" Strategy

Liquidate dead stock (e.g., Rocking Chairs) by bundling them with Star Products. Use recovered capital to reinvest in 'Custom' orders.

3

Diversify Supply Chain

Reduce dependency on "Sheesham Crafts Inc." by shifting 50% of orders to new, qualified local suppliers in Prayagraj.
